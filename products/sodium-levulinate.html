Sodium Levulinate

Mild, bio-based preservative and conditioning agent for clean, natural skin- and hair-care formulations.

Product overview

Sodium Levulinate (CAS No.: 539-88-8) is the sodium salt of levulinic acid, used as a natural-origin preservative and skin-conditioning agent in modern cosmetics and personal-care products. [web:21][web:22]

Main Applications

  • Broad-spectrum control of bacteria, yeasts, and molds in water-based formulations such as serums, gels, shampoos, and cleansers. [web:22][web:28]
  • Improves skin softness, smoothness, and hydration while supporting a healthy skin microbiome. [web:22][web:24]
  • Enhances hair feel and shine in shampoos, conditioners, and leave-on treatments. [web:23][web:31]
  • Often used in blends with Sodium Anisate for robust, globally acceptable preservation systems. [web:27][web:31][web:38]

Formulation Advantages

  • 100% plant-derived, vegan-friendly alternative to benzoic and sorbic acid preservatives. [web:22][web:37]
  • Highly effective in mildly acidic systems, typically between pH 4.0 and 6.0. [web:36][web:38]
  • Water-soluble and easy to incorporate into the aqueous phase without affecting product color or fragrance. [web:27][web:31]
