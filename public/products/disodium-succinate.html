Disodium Succinate Hexahydrate

White crystalline sodium salt of succinic acid — a versatile pH buffer, umami flavor enhancer, and sustainable chemical intermediate.

For R&D use only
Product overview

Disodium Succinate Hexahydrate (CAS 6106-21-4) is the sodium salt of succinic acid carrying six molecules of water of crystallisation. It is a white crystalline powder that is highly soluble in water, exhibits mild alkalinity (pH 7–9 in solution), and is biodegradable — particularly when sourced from fermentation-based succinic acid platforms.

Chemical Identity

CAS Number6106-21-4
Molecular FormulaC₄H₄Na₂O₄·6H₂O
Molecular Weight~270.14 g/mol
AppearanceWhite crystalline solid or powder
SolubilityHighly soluble in water; insoluble in most organic solvents
pH (1–5% solution)~7.0–9.0 (slightly alkaline)
OriginBio-based (fermentation-derived succinic acid)

Key Functional Properties

  • Buffering agent (pKa₂ ≈ 5.6 for the succinic acid system)
  • Chelating/stabilising behaviour toward certain metal ions
  • Mild alkalinity useful for pH adjustment in aqueous systems
  • Biodegradable and bio-based when derived from fermentation routes
  • Thermal stability: loses water of crystallisation on heating (~100–150 °C)

Main Applications

  • Food & Beverage: pH regulator, buffering agent, and umami/savory taste enhancer in seasonings, instant soups, and seafood processing.
  • Pharmaceuticals: Buffer component in formulations, IV fluids, and drug stabiliser; linked to Citric acid cycle intermediates.
  • Industrial & Chemical: Buffer solutions, chelating agent in metal processing and cleaning formulations, green chemistry intermediate.
  • Agriculture: Feed additive for metabolic support; component in specialty bio-based fertiliser formulations.

Handling & Storage

  • Store in cool, dry conditions — product is hygroscopic.
  • Avoid prolonged exposure to high temperatures (dehydration risk above ~100 °C).
  • Compatible with most aqueous systems; avoid strong oxidisers.
